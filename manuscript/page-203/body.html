Even so, some of the old-time micro people correctly questioned Dean’s style of “user friendliness.” )

In a Byte article, “Simplify, Simplify, Simplify,” Dean had pushed menu-driven software like Select.

Wasn’t that stretching it, however? If you ate at the same restaurant day after day, couldn’t you order many kinds of meals without bothering to glance at a menu? How would you feel about a waitress who insisted you see a menu even if the fare never changed? She would be pushy, wouldn’t she? All of a sudden “user friendliness” would be antiuser pushiness to those using a program regularly.
