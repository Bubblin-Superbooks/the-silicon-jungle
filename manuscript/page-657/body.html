With E-Mail you can:

  • Send messages computer to computer within an office or around the world. At their convenience recipients can flash messages across their screens or print them out.
  • Use a computer to zip messages to special centers near recipients. The centers print the messages, then deliver them by courier or local mail. That’s how you can overcome one of the original 229drawbacks of electronic mail—the other person’s lacking a computer on the same network.
  • Send and receive messages via the Telex network—which, of course, is why MCI Mail politely asked for “God’s” Telex number.
